# **Mycel Operating Doctrine (v0.1)**

*A practical path to nonviolent systemic change*

---

## **Purpose**

To enable people to meet real needs through local, voluntary cooperation—reducing dependence on centralized systems while increasing agency, trust, and resilience.

---

## **Core Principle**

> **Systems change when new behaviors become easier than old ones.**

Our task is not to convince people.
Our task is to make better ways of interacting **naturally attractive**.

---

## **Operating Strategy**

### 1. Start from reality, not ideology

Focus on **felt needs**, not abstract visions.

* Where are people underserved?
* What frustrates them in everyday life?

If it doesn’t solve a real problem, it won’t spread.

---

### 2. Build small loops of real value

Every effort must aim toward:

> **connect → see → act → something happens → repeat**

No loop = no system.

---

### 3. Keep participation effortless

Lower the threshold as much as possible:

* no belief required
* no complex onboarding
* no forced commitments
* easy to join, easy to leave

Adoption comes from **ease**, not persuasion.

---

### 4. Prioritize usefulness over completeness

Start with what works **now**, not what’s perfect.

* A simple meetup beats a full economic model
* One working interaction beats ten features

Reality is the only validator.

---

### 5. Let trust emerge before structure

Do not introduce:

* currencies
* rigid governance
* formal accounting

until people **naturally feel the need for them**

> **Do not formalize prematurely**

---

### 6. Use tools to reveal, not control

Tools (like Mycel) should:

* make people visible to each other
* surface opportunities
* reduce friction

They should not:

* dictate behavior
* track everything
* impose systems

---

### 7. Optimize for real-world action

Success is not:

* downloads
* features
* ideas

Success is:

> **people doing something together because of this**

---

### 8. Scale through replication

Do not aim for mass coordination.

Instead:

> **Make something small that works → let it spread**

Each successful micro-loop is a seed.

---

### 9. Follow incentives, not intentions

People don’t need to agree with you.

They need to:

> **benefit from participating**

If it improves their life, it grows.

---

### 10. Add complexity only when required

Only build new layers when reality demands it:

* communication → when coordination fails
* currency → when imbalance appears
* governance → when conflict emerges

> **Let problems pull solutions into existence**

---

## **Design Constraints (Non-Negotiable)**

* Low friction > high sophistication
* Real use > theoretical completeness
* Trust > metrics
* Action > discussion

---

## **Immediate Application**

Next step:

> Use Mycel to help **2–3 people from Gnistan meet outside once**

Nothing more.

That is the first real loop.

---

## **Guiding Question**

At all times, ask:

> **“Does this make it easier for people to do something real together?”**

If not, it’s not a priority.

---

## **Mantra**

> **Start small.
> Create real value.
> Keep it easy.
> Let trust grow.
> Repeat what works.**
