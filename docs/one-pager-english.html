Mycel
coordination that grows like a forest
the question

What if a small group of people could coordinate local life — tools, food, a storm warning — using a quiet app that then spread on its own to communities they'd never met, simply because it was useful?

the metaphor

Beneath every forest is a mycelial network — no center, no manager, no server. It routes nutrients from trees with surplus to trees in need, heals itself when damaged, and has maintained planetary resilience for hundreds of millions of years.

Mycel works the same way: connections strengthen where real exchange happens, atrophy where they don't, and keep routing locally when the internet fails. The forest doesn't need permission to grow.

alive in six months

A small group of people who know each other are using the first version. They can see a soft ambient map of who's around and what they need. They coordinate something real — tools, a meal, a response to a problem. The app gets out of the way afterward. It doesn't pull them back. It simply waits, like soil, until something needs to grow through it again. Someone shows it to people they know elsewhere. It spreads, one trusted edge at a time.

the anti-addiction test
The goal is a tool that people pick up when they need it and put down without difficulty — like a good shovel.
how it works
Two-speed signaling
🌿
Ambient layer — slow
A living map of presence, resource availability, community mood. Not a feed. No urgency. Updated gently, locally. Like knowing your neighbors' general state without constant contact.
⚡
Active layer — fast
Encrypted direct coordination — a need, an offer, a crisis signal. Appears when something actually requires action. Dissolves when it's done.
