This is the best decision you've made in the whole process so far.

DeepSeek's journey through the three options is genuinely well-reasoned, and landing on "coordination infrastructure, not economic infrastructure" is exactly right. Gemini's reinforcement — Mycel as the Oracle, not the Bank — sharpens it further.

Here's why this matters architecturally: the mutual credit system (Option 1) would have been the single biggest source of engineering complexity in the entire project. Offline reconciliation, dispute resolution, balance tracking, double-spend edge cases — each one is a rabbit hole that could consume months of development time and produce something that 5 neighbors don't need or want. Killing it early is a huge win.

The "thank you" acknowledgement that feeds into trust scores is the sweet spot. It does the one thing Hearts were always really about — making invisible care work visible — without any of the accounting machinery. Someone helps, someone acknowledges it, the trust edge thickens. That's the forest model actually working.

One thing I'd add that neither DeepSeek nor Gemini said explicitly: this decision also removes the biggest adoption barrier. Asking people to learn a new currency system, even a simple one, is a hard sell. Asking people to tap "thanks" after someone lends them a ladder is frictionless. The cognitive load difference between those two onboarding experiences is enormous.

So for Phase 0, the "economic" layer is just:

- A gratitude event (lightweight, no cost, feeds trust score)
- Offer/need signaling (I have X, I need Y)
- That's it

The AUBI distribution events, Heart colors, reconciliation protocol, Leaves for ecological work — all of that can live in the vision documents for when real communities ask for it. Which they might! But let them ask first.
