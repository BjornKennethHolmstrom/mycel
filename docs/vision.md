# Mycel
**A coordination substrate for living communities**

*Version 0.1 — March 2026*

---

## What it is

Mycel is a lightweight, offline-capable coordination network inspired by the architecture of mycelium — the underground fungal networks that connect forests, route nutrients between trees, and maintain planetary-scale resilience without any central organism in charge.

It is not a social media platform. It is not a replacement for the internet. It is infrastructure that quietly makes real-world coordination easier, works when the internet doesn't, and is designed to strengthen human relationships rather than substitute for them.

---

## The animating question

What would it look like if five neighbors could see each other's presence, signal needs, coordinate around a storm — and then six months later that same substrate had spread, organically, to communities they'd never met?

Not through marketing. Not through growth hacking. Simply because it was useful, and useful things spread like mycelium: quietly, along gradients of need.

---

## What alive looks like in six months

A small group of people who know each other are using the first version. They can see a soft ambient map of who's around, what they have to offer, what they need. They coordinate something real — tools, a shared meal, a response to a local problem. The app gets out of the way afterward. It doesn't pull them back in. It doesn't send notifications designed to trigger anxiety. It simply waits, like soil, until something needs to grow through it again.

Someone in that group shows it to people they know elsewhere. Those people install it. The network extends one trusted edge at a time.

## Architectural principles from nature

### Hyphal growth follows gradients, not plans
Mycelium doesn't map territory uniformly — it grows toward nutrients and withdraws from depleted areas. Mycel connections strengthen where meaningful exchange actually happens and quietly atrophy where they don't. No moderation team required. No algorithmic amplification. Just organic topology.

### Anastomosis: self-healing without coordination
When two hyphal threads meet, they can fuse, creating redundant paths around damage. Mycel nodes discover each other locally first — via Bluetooth, local Wi-Fi, mesh — and globally second. If a cable is cut or a platform is banned, the local mesh continues. The network heals itself without anyone deciding to heal it.

### Two speeds of signaling
Fungi use slow chemical gradients for ambient resource awareness and faster electrical signals for urgent response. Mycel mirrors this:

- **Ambient layer (slow):** A living map of presence, resource availability, and community mood. Not a feed. Updated slowly, locally. Useful for knowing your context without urgency.
- **Active layer (fast):** Encrypted direct coordination — a need, an offer, a crisis signal. Appears when something actually requires action. Disappears when it's done.

### Spore dispersal
A Mycel node should be a single lightweight executable that runs on a laptop, a Raspberry Pi, a phone. Someone in a new place can carry it on a USB stick, run it locally, and instantly have mesh with anyone nearby. No registration. No central server to ask permission from.

### Temporary constellations
When a few nodes coordinate intensely around something for a while, the architecture notices — the edges thicken, a temporary cluster becomes visible in the ambient map. When the activity ends, it fades. No ceremony. No admin roles. No group that needs to be formally disbanded. Like a cluster of fruiting bodies after rain: purposeful, temporary, leaving the network richer for having happened.

## What Mycel is not

**Not a Twitter clone.** It doesn't need network effects to be useful. It works for five people.

**Not a blockchain.** No global consensus, no energy waste, no immutable ledger conflicting with the right to be forgotten.

**Not a utopia.** The architecture enables mutual aid but doesn't guarantee it. People can still be selfish. But the design doesn't *prevent* cooperation the way current platforms do.

**Not a manifesto made technical.** The philosophy lives in the design, not in slogans. It should feel like infrastructure — earthy, practical, occasionally boring. The radical implications emerge from use, not from branding.

---

## Designed for real life at all scales

Drawing from Spiral Dynamics not as a formula but as a reminder: people coordinate from different motivational centers, and good infrastructure makes space for all of them without privileging any.

Basic needs (safety, food, shelter) are served by the offline mesh and local resource routing. Belonging emerges from persistent place-based nodes and ambient presence. Agency finds expression through urgent signaling that can amplify locally without overrunning the whole network. Order can be implemented locally through optional community charters — modular, not imposed. Efficiency is honored through resource matching and coordination tracking. Empathy flows through the ambient mood layer and slow conversation defaults. Systems thinkers find a neutral substrate that doesn't embed any single worldview. And the holistic impulse is served by the bioregional overlays and inter-cluster routing that connect local meshes into something planetary.

The architecture doesn't know which of these it's serving at any given moment. It just routes gradients.

---

## The anti-addiction design commitment

Every design decision is evaluated against a single question: does this strengthen or weaken real-world connection?

- No engagement-maximizing algorithms
- No notification systems designed to create compulsive checking — quiet by default
- No metrics that create status competition
- Heated coordination threads trigger designed friction, not amplification
- The app earns no revenue from attention

The goal is a tool that people pick up when they need it and put down without difficulty. Like a good shovel.

## Technical foundation

Built on **Nostr** as the base protocol — simple signed JSON events, decentralized relays, existing ecosystem — with Mycel adding:

- Trust-weighted adaptive topology (hyphal thickening based on meaningful interaction history)
- Ambient presence and resource gradient visualization
- Local mesh fallback via Bluetooth/Wi-Fi Direct when internet fails
- Optional Hearts/Love Ledger integration for resource routing and care acknowledgment
- Place-based node identity as an alternative to ego-based accounts

The underlying stack is chosen for longevity and legibility, not novelty.

## Relationship to the Global Governance Frameworks

Mycel is not the GGF made technical. But it is the substrate through which many GGF concepts — bioregional coordination, subsidiarity, the Social Fabric Framework, the Love Ledger — could become lived rather than theorized.

The Social Fabric Framework already knows the core principle: *create reasons to show up, and let belonging emerge from showing up.* Mycel is infrastructure built on exactly that insight. It creates the conditions. It doesn't engineer the outcome.

## What remains genuinely open

- **Place-based vs person-based identity:** Should nodes represent households and workshops, or individuals? This is a real architectural fork with significant social implications. Worth deciding slowly.
- **The first real community:** Who are the five neighbors? Where does the first real test happen?
- **Relationship to CivicBase/Swedish civil defense:** The municipal resilience framing isn't gone — it's a possible application layer, not the soul of the project.

---

## The name

**Mycel** — short for mycelium. Biologically resonant without being obscure. Evokes growth, underground connection, resilience without center. No tech-bro baggage.

The radical part is in the architecture. The name just needs to point at something real.
